About US.

We opened Forms 1 and 2 of Bidii Secondary School in 1994 - Form 3 in ’95, and form 4 in ’96. In January 2014 we opened the 2nd stream of Form 1 – 4 to cater for the influx of students getting admitted.

At the end of Form 4 a further National Government exam is sat, The Kenya Certificate of Secondary Education and this gives entrance to University for a further 4 years.

In 2012 we commenced providing 4 more classrooms, a library and Boarding Space for 60 more students.

In the year 2013 we built a swimming pool for swimming activities and sport making us one of the most unique schools in the region.

The first photo has been taken from the road, and shows the entrance gate to the School and the School Hall (right) The Hall seats 400 and has a stage. It is used for Gymnastics and Drama. The two Annual National Examinations are taken in the Hall as well. In July the Annual Parents & Prize Giving Day is held in the Hall, and students present a Concert.

Here we have walked in through the gate with the Hall on the right. The second building to the right is a computer laboratory. Computer is taught from form 1 through to form 4. Immediately in front of the Hall can be seen a low building which accommodates Junior and Senior staff rooms and Headmaster’s and other School Offices.

Taken from a different angle we now see the front of the building comprising teacher’s common rooms and offices. We have 32 qualified teachers (16 ladies and 16 men) who teach different disciplines in our secondary school. The principle and his deputy have their offices here, together with the school reception

Above is a view of the Daily Parade held in front of the Staff room block. This is held at 7.50 a.m. and prayers and a short address plus notices are given. On Fridays we raise the National Flag, sing the National Anthem and orally pledge loyalty to the President. All students and Staff attend. In the distance you can catch sight to the left School Labs for Science, Home Science, and Senior Computer.

Above is a view of one of a typical Classroom. We have students from all over Kenya, and they are not only African children, but also Asian. We have Hindus, Muslims, as well as children from Christian backgrounds We also have children from China, Korea, and even European backgrounds. Generally all students get on well together, and have happy time learning. Class lessons are From 8a.m. to 4p.m. in Primary.

FINALLY a group of our Form 4s during a period of study. We are glad to say that generally students in the School do well and go on to University and College. Many of our Old Boys and Girls from the School are now Doctors, Teachers, Lawyers, Architects, Dentists, Accountants, as well as a host of other professionals. Even more happily students leave us with a clear knowledge of God, and His Love, enabling them to face the world and its challenges with a Friend Who will always be there for them.
